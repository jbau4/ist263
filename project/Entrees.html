Crispy Rice with steamed Edamame

You will crave this dish. This dish will appear in your dreams. Resting on a ceramic plate is crisypy nigiri with spicy tuna atop and salty steamed edamame.

Rice Ingredients

  • Sushi rice
  • Sugar
  • Rice vinegar or white wine vinegar
  • Salt
  • Water
  • Sesame oil
Spicy Tuna Ingredients
  • Sushi grade ahi tuna
  • siracha
  • Mayonaise | Chef Reccomends Kewpie Mayo
  • Sesame Oil
  • Chives
  • Pepper
  • Salt
  • Garlic powder
Edamame Ingredients
  • Frozen edamame
  • Salt
Directions
  1. Rince your rice thoroughly and add to a pot with water.
  2. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and cover the pot. Let it cook for 15 minutes and then remove the heat and let it sit for 10 minutes.
  3. While the rice cooks, dice up your ahi tuna until its almost like a paste consistency. Then let it rest in the fridge for 10 minutes
  4. To a bowl, add your kewpie mayo, siracha, salt, pepper, drizzle of sesame oil, and garlic powder and mix to combine.
  5. Remove your tuna from the fridge and pour your siracha mayo on it and mix until it is well combined.
  6. Combine your rice vinegar, sugar, and salt in a small pot. Cook and mix it until all of the ingredients combine
  7. Remove your cooked rice to a seperate bowl and pour your rice vinegar mixture on it and mix thoroughly. It should be rather sticky now.
  8. Let the rice cool while you heat up a pan with a generouse amount of sesame oil (enough to fry)
  9. Form your cooled rice into whatever shape you want. It is best to form it into little rectangles.
  10. Once the oil is piping hot (check using a wooden chopstick if it bubbles it is ready to fry) add your rice rectangles to it and fry for about 2-3 minutes each side before flipping.
  11. While your rice fries, steam your frozen edamame, and then sprinkle it with salt.
  12. Once cooked, remove rice from the oil onto a tray lined with paper towel to absorb excess oil. Let it sit for 5 minutes.
  13. Finally, scoop about a table spoonful of your spicy tuna on top of each rice cake and sprinkle your chives on each one. Enjoy!

Chef Bau's Breakfast Mess

This dish is an original creation by Chef Bau himself. It is a hardy and delicious way to start your day.

Ingredients
  • Potatoes
  • Tomatos
  • Bacon
  • Parsely as garnish
  • eggs
  • onions
  • Parmeggiano reggiano
Directions
  1. Dice your onions and shred your potatos.
  2. Place your potatos in a cheese cloth and wring them out until they are completely dry.
  3. place your shredded potatos into a bowl, add your diced onions, parmesan cheese, and mix.
  4. In a medium sized pan, heat up a decent amount of olive oil on medium high heat.
  5. Place your potatos in the pan and shape them into a donut with an egg sized well int he center. Press down once shaped and let it crisp.
  6. Once golden on the bottom, flip.
  7. As the rest of the potatoes START to brown, crack an egg into that whole your created. Cover the pan so the top of the egg cooks.
  8. Prepare your toppings: Cook your bacon until desired crispyness, dice your tomatos, and chop your parsely.
  9. Once the egg looks cooked and the entire hashbrown is golden, remove from the pan and top with your toppings. Enjoy!
